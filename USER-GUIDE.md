# CreditFix - User Guide

Welcome! CreditFix helps you dispute inaccurate items on your credit report — completely free. Under federal law (the Fair Credit Reporting Act), you have the right to challenge anything on your credit report that's wrong. This app handles the hard part: figuring out what to dispute and writing the letters for you.

You print the letters and mail them yourself. That's it.

## 1. Getting Started

### Creating Your Account

1. Go to the CreditFix website
2. Click **Sign Up**
3. Enter your email and choose a password
4. Click **Create account**

That's it — you're in.

### Your Dashboard

After signing in, you'll land on your **Dashboard**. Think of this as your home base. It shows:

- How many disputes you have
- How many letters have been generated
- Any upcoming deadlines
- Quick links to your next steps

If this is your first time, the dashboard will be empty — that's normal! Follow the steps below to get started.

## 2. Setting Up Your Profile

**Do this first**, before generating any letters. Your name and address go on every dispute letter.

1. Click **Profile** in the top menu
2. Fill in:
   - **Full Name** — as it appears on your credit reports
   - **Street Address**
   - **City**, **State** (2-letter abbreviation like CA, TX, FL)
   - **ZIP Code**
3. Click **Save Profile**

You'll also see an option to set up **Email Reminders** on this page — more on that in [Section 10](#10-email-reminders).

## 3. Uploading Your Credit Reports

### Where to Get Your Reports

You're entitled to free credit reports from all three bureaus every year. Get them at:

**[annualcreditreport.com](https://www.annualcreditreport.com)**

Download the PDF version from each bureau:
- Experian
- Equifax
- TransUnion

**Important:** Download the *digital* PDF version. If you only have a printed/scanned copy, the app can't read it.

### Uploading

1. Click **Upload** in the top menu
2. You'll see three upload boxes — one per bureau
3. Either **drag and drop** your PDF onto the matching box, or **click the box** to browse your files
4. The app will show its progress:
   - **Uploading...** — sending the file
   - **Parsing report...** — reading the contents
   - **Done** — ready for analysis

You can upload one, two, or all three bureau reports — whatever you have.

### Running the AI Analysis

Once a report is uploaded and parsed, you'll see an **Analyze Report** button. Click it.

The AI will scan your report and identify items that may be inaccurate or disputable under federal law. This takes about 30 seconds per report.

When it's done, you'll see a green **Analysis complete** badge.

## 4. Reviewing Dispute Items

After analysis, go to the **Disputes** page (click **Disputes** in the top menu).

Here you'll see every item the AI flagged. For each item, you'll see:

- **Creditor name** — the company reporting the item
- **Account number** — just the last 4 digits (we never store full account numbers)
- **Dispute reason** — why this item may be inaccurate
- **FCRA section** — the specific law that applies
- **AI confidence** — how confident the AI is this item is disputable (shown as a percentage)

### What to Do

For each item, you have two choices:

- **Approve** — include this item in your dispute letters
- **Skip** — leave this item alone for now

Go through each item and approve the ones you want to dispute. If you're unsure, items with higher confidence scores (70%+, shown in green) are stronger candidates.

You can filter by bureau using the tabs at the top of the page.

### Generating Letters

Once you've approved your items, scroll down. You'll see a blue banner showing how many items are approved. Click **Generate Letters**.

The AI will write a personalized dispute letter for each bureau, citing the specific laws and reasons for each item.

## 5. Generating and Downloading Letters

Go to the **Letters** page (click **Letters** in the top menu).

Your letters are grouped by bureau. For each letter, you can:

- **Download PDF** — saves the letter to your computer, ready to print
- **Preview** — see the letter content right in your browser
- **Mark as Sent** — record when you mailed it (more on this below)

The downloaded PDF includes:
- Your name and address (from your Profile)
- The bureau's mailing address
- Today's date
- Each disputed item with the specific reason and FCRA citation
- A request for investigation and correction

## 6. Printing and Mailing Your Letters

This is the one step you do yourself — and it's important to do it right.

### What You Need

- A printer
- Envelopes
- **USPS Certified Mail** (this gives you proof the bureau received your letter)

### How to Mail

1. **Print** each letter
2. Take them to your local **USPS post office**
3. Send each letter via **Certified Mail with Return Receipt**
   - This costs about $4-5 per letter
   - You'll get a tracking number on a green receipt
   - The post office will give you proof of mailing
4. **Keep your tracking numbers** — you'll need them

### Bureau Mailing Addresses

The letters already include the correct addresses, but for reference:

| Bureau | Address |
|---|---|
| **Experian** | P.O. Box 4500, Allen, TX 75013 |
| **Equifax** | P.O. Box 740256, Atlanta, GA 30374 |
| **TransUnion** | P.O. Box 2000, Chester, PA 19016 |

### After Mailing

Come back to the **Letters** page and click **Mark as Sent** for each letter:

1. Enter the **date you mailed it**
2. Enter your **Certified Mail tracking number** (optional but recommended)
3. Click **Confirm Sent**

This starts the 30-day countdown. By law, bureaus must respond within 30 days.

## 7. Tracking Your Disputes

After marking letters as sent, go to the **Tracker** page.

This is where you monitor your disputes. Each card shows:

- The **creditor name** and **bureau**
- **Days remaining** until the 30-day deadline
- The date you sent the letter
- Your tracking number

### Color Coding

Cards change color based on urgency:

| Color | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Blue** | Active — still within the 30-day window |
| **Yellow/Amber** | Approaching — 5 or fewer days left |
| **Red** | Overdue — the bureau hasn't responded in 30 days |
| **Green** | Resolved — the item was removed or corrected |

### Filter Tabs

Use the tabs at the top to filter:
- **All** — everything
- **Active** — disputes still waiting for a response
- **Overdue** — disputes past the 30-day deadline
- **Resolved** — completed disputes

## 8. Recording Bureau Responses

When a bureau sends you a response letter (usually by mail), come back to the Tracker and record it.

1. Find the dispute in the **Tracker**
2. Click **Record Response**
3. You have two options:

### Option A: Upload Their Response PDF

If the bureau emailed or mailed you a PDF:
1. Click the **Upload PDF** tab
2. Select the file
3. Click **Upload & Parse**
4. The AI will read the response and extract the outcome

### Option B: Enter Manually

If you received a paper letter:
1. Click the **Enter Manually** tab
2. Select the outcome:
   - **Item Deleted** — the item was removed from your report (this is a win!)
   - **Item Corrected** — the item was updated/fixed
   - **Bureau Verified (Denied)** — the bureau says the item is accurate
   - **No Response Received** — the deadline passed with no reply
3. Optionally add any notes or reason codes from their letter
4. Click **Save Response**

## 9. Escalating When Needed

Sometimes the first letter isn't enough. CreditFix gives you two escalation options:

### Demand Letter (When They Don't Respond)

If a bureau misses the 30-day deadline:
1. A **Generate Demand Letter** button will appear on that dispute's Tracker card
2. Click it — the AI writes a stronger follow-up letter citing the bureau's failure to respond within the legally required timeframe
3. Download, print, and mail it the same way as before (Certified Mail)

### CFPB Complaint (When They Deny Your Dispute)

If a bureau says the item is accurate and you disagree:
1. A **Generate CFPB Narrative** button will appear
2. Click it — the AI writes a formal complaint narrative for filing with the **Consumer Financial Protection Bureau** (CFPB)
3. Click **File at CFPB** — this opens the government complaint portal
4. Paste the generated narrative into the CFPB form
5. Track the status in the app (Draft, Filed, Response Received, Closed)

Filing a CFPB complaint is free and puts additional pressure on the bureau to investigate. The government reviews these complaints and holds bureaus accountable.

## 10. Email Reminders

CreditFix can email you when deadlines are approaching so you don't have to check the app constantly.

### Setting Up Reminders

1. Go to **Profile**
2. Scroll down to **Email Reminders**
3. Check **Enable email reminders**
4. Optionally enter a different email address (otherwise it uses your login email)
5. Click **Save Preferences**

### When You'll Get Emails

| When | What |
|---|---|
| **Day 25** | "Deadline approaching" — the bureau has ~5 days left to respond |
| **Day 31** | "No response" — the deadline has passed, time to take action |

Each email includes a link back to the Tracker so you can take next steps.

## 11. Frequently Asked Questions

### Is this legal?

Yes. The Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA) gives every American the right to dispute inaccurate information on their credit report. This app simply helps you exercise that right. You're writing letters and mailing them yourself — nothing is done on your behalf.

### How much does this cost?

The app is free. Your only costs are printing and postage (roughly $4-5 per letter for Certified Mail).

### How long does the whole process take?

- **Setting up and sending first letters:** About 30 minutes
- **Bureau response deadline:** 30 days from when they receive your letter
- **Full process (if escalation needed):** 2-3 months total

### What if I have items on all three bureau reports?

Upload all three reports. The app handles each bureau separately — you'll get individual letters for each one.

### Do I need to dispute the same item with all three bureaus?

Only if the item appears on multiple bureau reports. The AI will flag items per bureau, so you'll know which bureaus are reporting each issue.

### What's Certified Mail and why does it matter?

Certified Mail gives you proof that the bureau received your letter. Without it, the bureau could claim they never got it. The green receipt from the post office and the tracking number are your proof.

### What should I do while waiting for a response?

Just wait. The bureau has 30 days to investigate and respond. The app will email you when the deadline is approaching (if you set up reminders). Don't send additional letters during this time — it can restart the 30-day clock.

### What counts as a "win"?

If the bureau **deletes** the item or **corrects** it in your favor, that's a win. This can improve your credit score, sometimes significantly. Items that are deleted or corrected will no longer appear on your credit report.

### What if the bureau verifies the item?

This means the bureau investigated and says the information is accurate. You have two options:
1. **Generate an Escalation Letter** — a stronger follow-up
2. **File a CFPB Complaint** — escalate to the federal government

Many items that are initially "verified" get removed after a CFPB complaint.

### Can I dispute items again later?

Yes. You can always upload new reports and run the analysis again. Credit reports change over time, and new issues may appear.

1. **Sign up** and **set up your Profile** (name + address)
2. **Download** your free credit reports from [annualcreditreport.com](https://www.annualcreditreport.com)
3. **Upload** them to CreditFix
4. **Run AI Analysis** on each report
5. **Review and Approve** the flagged items
6. **Generate Letters**
7. **Download and Print** the letters
8. **Mail via Certified Mail** (USPS)
9. **Mark as Sent** in the app with your tracking numbers
10. **Wait 30 days** (the app tracks the deadline for you)
11. **Record the response** when the bureau replies
12. **Escalate if needed** (demand letter or CFPB complaint)

That's it. You've got this!
